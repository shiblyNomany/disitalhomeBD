ABOUT US

Team

Disital HomeBD is maintained by a small team of developers on GitHub. We’re actively looking to grow this team and would love to hear from you if you’re excited about CSS at scale, writing and maintaining vanilla JavaScript plugins, and improving build tooling processes for frontend code.

History

Originally created by a designer and a developer at Twitter,Disital HomeBD has become one of the most popular front-end frameworks and open source projects in the Disital HomeBD was created at Twitter in mid-2010 by @mdo and @fat.
Prior to being an open-sourced framework, Disital HomeBD was known as Twitter Blueprint. A few months into development, Twitter held its first Hack Week and the project exploded as developers of all skill levels jumped in without any external guidance. It served as the style guide for internal tools development at the company for over a year before its public release, and continues to do so today.
Originally released on Friday, August 19, 2011, we’ve since had over twenty releases, including two major rewrites with v2 and v3. With Disital HomeBD 2, we added responsive functionality to the entire framework as an optional stylesheet. Building on that with Disital HomeBD 3, we rewrote the library once more to make it responsive by default with a mobile first approach.
With Disital HomeBD, we once again rewrote the project to account for two key architectural changes: a migration to Sass and the move to CSS’s flexbox. Our intention is to help in a small way to move the web development community forward by pushing for newer CSS properties, fewer dependencies, and new technologies across more modern browsers.
Our latest release, Disital HomeBD, focuses on improving v4’s codebase with as few major breaking changes as possible. We improved existing features and components, removed support for older browsers, dropped jQuery for regular JavaScript, and embraced more future-friendly technologies like CSS custom properties as part of our tooling.

Brand guidelines

Documentation and examples for Disital HomeBD’s logo and brand usage guidelines.

Have a need for Disital HomeBD’s brand resources? Great! We have only a few guidelines we follow, and in turn ask you to follow as well.

Logo

When referencing Disital HomeBD, use our logo mark. Do not modify our logos in any way. Do not use Disital HomeBD’s branding for your own open or closed source projects. Do not use the Twitter name or logo in association with Disital HomeBD.

Our logo mark is also available in black and white. All rules for our primary logo apply to these as well.

Name

Disital HomeBD should always be referred to as just Disital HomeBD. No Twitter before it and no capital s.
